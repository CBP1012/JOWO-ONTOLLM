Call for Papers: Promoting Healthy Aging through Semantic Enrichment of Solitude Research (PHASES)

Challenges

Research on solitude often highlights risks such as mortality and depression. When positive outcomes are found, they are typically strongest among midlife and older adults. Similarly, research on gerotranscendence, which emphasizes psychological connectedness in aging, focuses on positive aging outcomes. Despite their overlap, these literatures remain disconnected, partly due to ambiguous terminology and the complexity of integrating constructs across studies. By bridging solitude and gerotranscendence research, we can deepen our understanding of solitudeâ€™s role in healthy aging and inspire new research directions. Our project, PHASES, seeks to unify these fields through ontology development and semantic enrichment.

Our Contributions

We will develop the Solitude Ontology and the Gerotranscendence Ontology, both extending from the Behavior Change Intervention Ontology. Beyond ontology creation, we will provide:

  • A web portal for automated literature recommendations and natural language query interfaces.
  • A hub for researchers, educators, and professionals to explore solitude and gerotranscendence concepts.
  • Training materials for graduate and undergraduate students on ontology use.

Related Work

Despite existing research in solitude and gerotranscendence, no unified framework currently integrates these perspectives. Our work extends beyond prior studies by developing structured ontologies and making this knowledge accessible via semantic web technologies.

Purpose of Our Call

We invite researchers to contribute papers on topics including but not limited to:

  • Empirical studies on solitude and aging.
  • Ontology engineering for behavioral sciences.
  • Semantic web applications in psychology and aging research.
  • Interdisciplinary approaches to healthy aging.
